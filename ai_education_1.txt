Generative AI is transforming the landscape of modern education. Students can now access personalized learning experiences at any time. Large language models can generate essays, solve problems, and answer complex questions. This shift raises fundamental questions about the future of traditional schooling.

Classrooms are no longer the sole space where knowledge is acquired and exchanged. Digital platforms powered by generative AI provide students with instant feedback on their work. The boundary between formal and informal learning is becoming increasingly blurred. Educators must adapt their teaching strategies to integrate AI tools effectively.

The role of the teacher is evolving from knowledge transmitter to learning facilitator. Students are encouraged to explore topics independently with the assistance of intelligent systems. Critical thinking skills are more important than ever in an AI-assisted world. Schools must rethink their curricula to prepare students for a future shaped by intelligent machines.

Assessment methods designed decades ago are struggling to remain relevant today. A student who can skillfully prompt an AI system demonstrates a new and valuable form of literacy. Institutions must distinguish between using AI as a crutch and using it as a genuine cognitive tool. The definition of academic competence is being quietly but profoundly rewritten.
