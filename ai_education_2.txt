Generative AI is transforming the landscape of modern education. Students can now access personalized learning experiences at any time. Large language models can generate essays, solve problems, and answer complex questions. This shift raises fundamental questions about the future of traditional schooling.

Academic integrity is a growing concern in the age of generative AI. Many students use AI tools to complete assignments without fully engaging with the material. Institutions are struggling to design assessments that accurately measure genuine understanding. New detection tools are being developed, but they remain imperfect and controversial.

Universities are investing in AI literacy programmes to equip students with responsible usage skills. Educators must adapt their teaching strategies to integrate AI tools effectively. Faculty members are divided between embracing these technologies and resisting their adoption. The conversation around AI in academia is no longer optional but urgently necessary.

Generative AI introduces both unprecedented opportunities and serious ethical dilemmas for educators. Personalised tutoring systems can support learners who would otherwise be left behind in a traditional classroom. Critical thinking skills are more important than ever in an AI-assisted world. The question is not whether AI will change education, but how quickly institutions will choose to respond.
