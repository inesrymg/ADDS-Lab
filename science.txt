The sun is a star at the center of our solar system.
It provides light and heat to all the planets around it.
Without the sun, life on Earth would not exist.

Plants use sunlight to produce energy through photosynthesis.
They absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen into the air.
This process is essential for all living organisms on Earth.

Water covers more than seventy percent of the Earth surface.
It exists in three states: liquid, solid, and gas.
The water cycle moves water between the ocean, atmosphere, and land.
