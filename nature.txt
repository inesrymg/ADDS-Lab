The ocean is home to millions of species of plants and animals.
It regulates the Earth climate and produces most of its oxygen.
Life in the ocean depends on sunlight and water temperature.

Forests cover about thirty percent of the land on Earth.
Trees absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen into the air.
Many animals depend on forests for food and shelter.

The water cycle is driven by the energy of the sun.
Water evaporates from the ocean and falls as rain on land.
Rivers carry the water back to the ocean completing the cycle.
